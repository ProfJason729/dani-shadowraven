Dani Shadowmouse
The Transformation of Yesterhill

A Curse of Strahd personal quest.

The night presses close. Ravens circle. Your fate waits at the summit of Yesterhill — written in shadow, blood, and the choices only you can make.

What you carry into the dark, and what you leave behind, will define who you become.

Section I — The Road to Yesterhill

As the afternoon sun casts long shadows over the Wizard of Wines Winery, you, Dani Shadowmouse, feel the weight of the coming night press against your spirit. The Holy Symbol of Ravenkind, now safely tucked in your pocket, pulses with a faint warmth against your thigh — a constant reminder of the promise and peril that lies ahead.

With Elvir, Stephania, and young Viggo Martikov at your side, you step beyond the familiar rows of grapevines. The Martikovs, clad in robes of deep gray embroidered with raven feathers, speak little, their faces etched with solemn determination. Viggo's small hand occasionally brushes against yours, a silent plea for reassurance you cannot refuse.

Ravens flit overhead, their black silhouettes stark against the dimming sky. As Yesterhill rises ominously before you, its silhouette a dark stain against the sky, their calls fill the air — a cacophony that seems to both warn and welcome.

"We are watched, but not just by the living. Remember, Dani, every step you take tonight carves deeper into your fate."

Stephania's gaze lingers on the fluttering ravens, her lips moving in a silent prayer. Her hand reaches out to touch a pendant at her neck, shaped like a raven in flight, and you feel a surge of resolve. As the quartet ascends to the ancient altar at the base of Yesterhill, a chill breeze whispers through the trees.

The air thickens with the scent of moss and age-old secrets as the spirit of Kavan, the barbarian chieftain, materializes before you — his spectral form marked by the regalia of his tribe: fur, bone, and the remnants of a warrior's armor.

"Listen well, Dani Shadowmouse, for your path intertwines with the legacy of hunters old and new. Solve this riddle to prove your understanding of the forces that bind your fate."
I am not seen but hold many together,
Twisting, turning, through all kinds of weather.
From vine to cloak, from shadow to light,
In the hands of fate, I pull threads tight.

What am I?

Section II — The Summit of Yesterhill

As you traverse the steep, slippery path through the dense woods of Yesterhill, the air grows colder, thick with the scent of impending rain. The sky above churns ominously, dark clouds roiling as occasional flashes of lightning illuminate your way.

Passing the concentric rings of ancient burial mounds — cairns of slimy black rocks piled ominously high — you feel the silent weight of history. At the summit, the remnants of a once towering fifty-foot statue sprawl across the druids' circle.

Towards the southern end of this haunted summit, a sickly copse beckons — a grove of dead trees with the monstrous, twisted Gulthias Tree at its heart. Blood-like sap oozes from its trunk, and around it, needle-covered humanoid creatures lurk ominously.

Despite the unsettling scene, Elvir and Stephania begin the ritual, laying out the Holy Symbol of Ravenkind on a makeshift altar formed from the intertwining roots of the Gulthias Tree. As they chant ancient words, the symbols begin to glow softly, casting eerie, dancing shadows.

"Dani, the choice of how you confront this transformation is yours alone. Will you allow the darkness of your past to be part of your strength, or will you seek purification, hoping to cleanse your curse entirely?"
How do you face your transformation?
Section III — Embrace the Shadows

As the ritual nears its peak, the darkness deepens — a tangible presence caressing the twisted roots of the Gulthias Tree. The Holy Symbol of Ravenkind and the ancient raven carving glow more intensely, casting long, sinuous shadows that intertwine like the threads of fate.

From the depths of the shadows, a figure emerges, shrouded in tattered robes that seem woven from the night itself. Her eyes, clouded yet piercing, lock onto you with a scrutiny that feels almost physical.

"Dani Shadowmouse. On the night your village fell, you faced a choice that defined you. In the terror and chaos, as you tried to save your brother, what drove you? Was it love, desperation, or the refusal to succumb to fate?"
What drove you that night?
Section III — Seek a New Dawn

The light from the Holy Symbol of Ravenkind spreads, bathing the clearing in a luminous glow that pushes back against the encroaching darkness. From a profound silence, a figure emerges draped in robes that shimmer with the light of dawn — The Huntress, her form timeless, woven from the light and dark of countless dawns.

"Dani Shadowmouse. You witnessed the destruction of everything you held dear, yet here you stand, seeking light. Tell me — on the night of the attack, what was the spark that kept hope alive within you, even as the shadows fell?"
What kept hope alive?
Section III — Balance Both Forces

The ritual finds its rhythm between shadow and light. From a balanced stillness, a figure emerges — The Huntress, appearing as a fusion of light and shadow, robes flowing with patterns of both night and day, eyes holding the depth of twilight skies.

"Dani Shadowmouse. Your life has been a tapestry of light and darkness, each thread as vital as the next. On that fateful night, what kept you from falling completely into despair or blind rage? What balance did you find within yourself?"
What balance did you find?

The Price of Shadows

The energies climax, swirling around you in a whirlwind of shadow and light. The transformation is profound — your body reshapes, senses sharpen, newfound agility infusing your every movement.

However, as the transformation settles, a cherished memory begins fading, slipping through your fingers like sand. A particularly beloved moment from your past becomes hazy, lost to the ether.

This memory will be chosen in consultation with your DM.
"How do you feel now, Dani Shadow... raven? Are you... different?"

Mimicry
The wereraven can mimic simple sounds it has heard, such as a person whispering, a baby crying, or an animal chattering. A creature that hears the sounds can tell they are imitations with a successful DC 10 Wisdom (Insight) check.
Beak (Raven or Hybrid Form Only)
Melee Weapon Attack: +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 1 piercing damage in raven form, or 4 (1d4 + 2) piercing damage in hybrid form. If the target is a humanoid, it must succeed on a DC 10 Constitution saving throw or be cursed with wereraven lycanthropy.
Change Shape
The wereraven polymorphs into a raven–humanoid hybrid or into a Tiny raven, or back into its humanoid form. Its statistics, other than its size and speed, are the same in each form. Any equipment it is wearing or carrying isn't transformed. It reverts to its humanoid form if it dies.

Speed: 25 ft. humanoid  ·  10 ft., fly 50 ft. raven  ·  30 ft., fly 50 ft. hybrid
Enhanced Lucky
When you roll a 1 on a d20 for an attack roll, ability check, or saving throw, you can reroll the die as well as an extra d8; you must use the new roll.

Yet the cost is immediate and profound. A significant ability that once defined your prowess dims significantly — its loss the price of what you have gained.

This loss will be determined in consultation with your DM.
